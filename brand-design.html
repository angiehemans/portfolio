Hemans Brewing

Family Craft Beer Label Design

Role: Designer Type: Personal Project

The Project

A family homebrewing venture turned into a passion project for brand and packaging design. My brother, dad, and I started brewing our own beer, and I took the opportunity to design a complete label system for our brews. The project has evolved over the years as we've refined both the beer and the branding.

What I Did

  • Designed complete label system for multiple beer varieties
  • Created cohesive brand identity across all packaging
  • Developed packaging templates for future brews
  • Iterated on designs through multiple brewing seasons

My Darling Mayday

Band Identity & Merchandise Design

Role: Designer Type: Personal Project

The Project

My Darling Mayday was a Pop-Punk band from Stockton, California. After a five-year hiatus, the group reformed for select dates and needed a fresh visual identity. I was tasked with creating designs for their unreleased album "Just Lay Still" and a full range of promotional materials.

What I Did

  • Designed album artwork for "Just Lay Still"
  • Created merchandise line including shirts, stickers, and buttons
  • Developed CD packaging and physical album materials
  • Branded band equipment including drum kit graphics

About

I'm Angie, a design leader who still loves to make things.

I've spent the last decade at the intersection of design and engineering, leading teams while staying hands-on with the work. I've scaled a design org from 1 to 8, shipped products that grew user bases by tens of thousands, and built design systems from the ground up. I often implement them in code myself.

My path to design leadership started with earning my BFA in Graphic Design from Pacific Union College, where I focused on brand identity and interactive design. That foundation has influenced my work as an interface and digital product designer over the years, shaping how I approach both visual systems and user experiences.

I learned early on that great design doesn't exist in a vacuum. It lives in the code, in the systems, and in the way teams collaborate to ship. That's why I've always stayed close to implementation. Today, I'm most energized by the challenge of building something meaningful. Whether that's a product, a team, or a system that helps both thrive, I want to be in the work, not just directing it from the sidelines.
